Cognitive rehabilitation, in your language.

ReTrace helps adults recovering from stroke, brain injury and other neurological conditions practise memory, attention, executive function and processing speed — every exercise tied to something real in daily life.

Request early access See the family of apps

What it does

Six domains, ten levels each, adapting independently to where you are this week.

Working memory

Holding a phone number, a code or a short list in mind.

Attention

Staying focused while distractions happen around you.

Processing speed

Thinking and reacting a little faster.

Executive function

Planning and ordering everyday tasks.

Everyday memory

Remembering messages, lists and appointments.

Visual memory

Remembering where things are and what you saw.

Built around how recovery actually works

  • Every exercise is framed around a daily-life situation, not a puzzle for its own sake.
  • Independent difficulty tracking per domain — strong areas don't pull weaker ones along.
  • Content generated in your language, including Welsh and Irish.
  • High contrast, larger text, reduced motion and spoken instructions, available from day one.
  • Clinician mode: assign only the modules a patient needs, and read the progress report.

For clinicians and organisations

ReTrace is designed as a clinical adjunct — a tool that complements care, not a substitute for it. Organisation licensing, clinician dashboards and patient assignment are part of the roadmap. If you'd like a briefing or a pilot, get in touch.
